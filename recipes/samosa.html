Samosa

Description

Samosa, an iconic snack originating from Indian cuisine, is a globally cherished treat. With its thin, crispy exterior enveloping a flavorful filling of spiced potatoes and peas, the triangular pastry offers a delightful blend of textures. Evolving into various regional variations, the samosa has become a popular street food and a staple in festive celebrations.

Crafted with precision, samosas involve a dough made from flour, water, and oil, shaped around a filling of spiced potatoes, peas, and aromatic spices. Deep-fried to golden perfection, the result is a crispy outer layer complementing the savory interior. Served with tamarind chutney or mint sauce, samosas symbolize culinary diversity, pleasing taste buds worldwide.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour, Water, Salt, Cooking Oil
  • Potatoes, Peas, Spices (cumin seeds, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric, chili powder)
  • Ginger, Green Chilies, Coriander Leaves, Cooking Oil (for sautéing)
  • Minced Meat (lamb or chicken)
  • Cooking Oil (for deep frying)
  • Tamarind Chutney, Mint Chutney, Yogurt

Steps

  1. Mix flour, salt, and oil; add water and rest.
  2. Boil, mash potatoes; sauté spices, peas, and optional meat.
  3. Roll dough, cut semi-circles; form cones, fill with mixture.
  4. USeal edges, deep-fry until golden brown.
  5. Enjoy hot with chutney or yogurt.
  6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll.
